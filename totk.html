BACKGROUND

BREATH OF THE WILD AND PLACEMENT IN THE ZELDA TIMELINE

Based on statements in the game, the two most logical places on the Zelda Timeline that TOTK's past time period could take place are either after every other game but before BOTW, or in between Skyward Sword and The Minish Cap. There is one important piece of lore that is imperative to consider when talking about TOTK's past: the founding of Hyrule.

When Rauru arrives from the sky and creates his kingdom in TOTK's past, he founds the Kingdom of Hyrule alongside Sonia. Rauru being the first king of Hyrule has interesting consequences in regard to the timeline. The only other times Hyrule was founded in the timeline was after Skyward Sword, before the timeline split, and after Phantom Hourglass, on the Adult Timeline.

Rauru in TOTK founding Hyrule after Phantom Hourglass isn't possible because it’s Link and Zelda who founded that new version of the Kingdom in the Adult Timeline. However, because the actual founding of a kingdom named Hyrule was never shown in Skyward Sword, but rather occurred slightly later on the timeline, it’s at least possible that TOTK's past could take place there.

On the other hand, there's the possibility BOTW and TOTK take place much further into the future from every other Zelda game than fans realize. If that's the case, then the 10,000 years of time that Princess Zelda travels back to in TOTK could theoretically still occur after every other game. This would mean that Rauru's "founding" of Hyrule is actually a refounding of a new Hyrule after the one from prior games was somehow lost to time.

PART I

THE FALL THAT LED TO A TRAGEDY

The appointed royal knight, Link, and Zelda, princess of Hyrule, set out to explore a cavern beneath Hyrule Castle, from which a poisonous substance called "gloom" has been seeping out and causing the people of Hyrule to fall ill. Here , they find historical murals depicting the founding of Hyrule and a subsequent conflict known as the Imprisoning War—an ancient battle against the "Demon King", whom sought to rule Hyrule under his evil reign—which Zelda believes to be related to the mysterious Zonai race.

Upon venturing deeper, Link and Zelda discover a mummy being restrained by a disembodied arm. The mummy awakens and attacks the princess and her knight. In the aftermath of the scuffle, Link's right arm is wounded, and the Master Sword is shattered by the power of the mummy and disappears into a portal.Hyrule Castle is then raised into the sky, while Zelda falls into the depths below; as Link tries to catch her, she vanishes along with a mysterious glimmering artifact. Link is rescued by the arm, awakening later on the Great Sky Island to find that it has replaced his previously damaged limb.

PART II

OLD AND NEW FRIENDS THAT LEAD THE WAY

Here, he meets the spirit of Rauru, part of the Zonai race and the source of his new arm, who helps him traverse the Great Sky Island. Once Link reaches his destination, the shattered Master Sword vanishes and he returns to the surface below. Link then learns that the event in the cavern, known as the Upheaval, has wrought chaos upon Hyrule, and he sets out to investigate reports of strange phenomena throughout the kingdom involving the gloom.

Link ventures into ancient Zonai temples after reuniting with his allies; Sidon, the prince of the zora and, later, king of the Zora; Tulin, a young Rito archer and son of Teba, a past ally of Link's; Yunobo, a Goron, now the president of the YunoboCo mining company; and Riju, the young chief of the Gerudo. After defeating the various monsters that have taken over the temples, the group finds artifacts called secret stones and meet the spirits of ancient sages, who appoint Link's allies as their successors and pass down their secret stones and special abilities.

PART III

LIES AND MIRAGES OF THE ENEMY

After numerous reported sightings of Zelda throughout Hyrule, Link finally catches up to her at Hyrule Castle, where she reveals herself to be an illusion created by Phantom Ganon, a servant of the Demon King, Ganondorf, the mummy who Link previously encountered. Link defeats the imposter Demon King and sets out to find Mineru, the last of the ancient sages, who remains in the physical world via spiritual projection.

He has to assemble a mechanical body for her spirit to inhabit to better reach her and speak of Hyrule's history. Through Mineru, the ancient sages, and Dragon's Tears (small glowing stones scattered across hyrule that reveal integral memories to the mystery of where Zelda has gone), Link finally learns of Zelda's fate.

PART IIII

HYRULE OF THE PAST AND PRESENT

The artifact she encountered when she made her fall through the chasm was a secret stone that transported her to the distant past, where she met Rauru, Hyrule's first king, and Sonia, its first queen. She quickly bonded with them and saw them as the parental figures she never had. Here while Zelda is in the past, Ganondorf tragically murders Sonia and uses her secret stone to become the Demon King.

In response, Rauru appoints his older sister Mineru, Zelda, and the leaders of the Zora, Rito, Gorons, and Gerudo as sages. With their help, Rauru fought Ganondorf and sacrificed himself, to seal the demon king away as the result of a devastating battle between the group. However, it is said that this seal will not last forever. After this battle, princess Zelda, still in the past, receives the shattered Master Sword from the future and tasks the sages with aiding Link once Ganondorf reawakened, which they followed as previously discussed.

PART V

SACRIFICES AND DRAGONIFICATION

However, in order to repair the Master Sword and bring it to Link in the present, Zelda makes the sacrifice of a lifetime; she swallows her secret stone and undergoes the process of "dragonification" becoming the immortal Light Dragon and losing her sense of self, as it's said it cannot ever be reversed.

In the present, after ridding the Great Deku Tree from gloom in Korok Forest, Link retrieves the Master Sword encased in the Light Dragon's crest, and goes below Hyrule Castle to confront Ganondorf.

PART VI

BATTLE AND TRIUMPH

With aid from Sidon, Tulin, Yunobo, Riju, and Mineru, Link battles an army of monsters before engaging in combat with Ganondorf himself. Nearing defeat, Ganondorf swallows his secret stone, becoming the Demon Dragon in a final attempt to kill Link. With help from the Light Dragon, aka Zelda, Link uses the newly repaired Master Sword to shatter the Demon Dragon's secret stone, killing him.

The spirits of Rauru and Sonia help Link return Zelda to normal, which is unheard of and restore his right arm before fading away; he and Zelda fall to the surface in eachothers arms, where they reunite. Some time later, on the Great Sky Island, Mineru bids farewell to Zelda and Link before fading away, while the new sages vow to protect Hyrule.
